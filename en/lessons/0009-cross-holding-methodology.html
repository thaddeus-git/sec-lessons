Cross-Holding Analysis Methodology
What questions does this report answer, and how to read it

The previous 3 lessons (13F / Form 4 / 13D-G) covered all data sources for cross-holding analysis. This lesson returns to methodology -- what principles should guide us when assembling these data into a report?

Our reference is the IHS Markit P4 report (Cross Holding / Peer Comparison). Our Dashboard + Markdown report is a replication, but narrower in scope and less automated. The essence is learning its design philosophy, not replicating every single page.

Core design philosophy: A good cross-holding report does not "dump all data on one page." Instead, each page answers one specific investment question. When a reader turns to a page, they should have a corresponding question in mind -- "What answer am I looking for?"

Each page answers one specific investment question

The IHS Markit P4 report has 10 pages, each mapped to a clear investment question. Below is the complete "page-question-use" mapping:

PageTable/ChartInvestment Question AnsweredHow It Is Used
P2Top Holders PositionsWho holds large positions in us and our competitors?Identify "smart money" allocation -- which large institutions hold both us and competitors
P3Top Holders ChangesAre these large institutions increasing or decreasing?Judge marginal changes in institutional sentiment -- more informative than static holdings
P4ActivistsWhich activist investors are watching us or our competitors?Early warning of potential activist campaigns
P5Top BuyersWho is actively buying into this competitive space?Discover new capital entering -- could signal someone is bullish on the industry
P6Top SellersWho is exiting this competitive space?Early warning of capital outflow -- could signal deteriorating industry fundamentals
P7Initiations / LiquidationsWho just entered or completely exited?Most extreme signals -- new position = strongly bullish, liquidation = strongly bearish
P8Active/Passive OrientationAre holdings mainly active management or passive tracking?More passive money = changes may be index rebalancing
P9Investment Style ClassificationAre holders value or growth investors?Value vs growth rotation reflects the company's lifecycle stage
P10Turnover DistributionAre holders trading frequently or holding long-term?High-frequency traders = noisy signal; long-term holders = strong signal

Signal stacking across pages (important)

A single page's signal has limited value. True insight comes from cross-page cross-validation. Below are 4 classic signal stacking patterns:

Scenario 1: Confirmatory signal (bullish)

P2: An institution is the Top 1 holder
P3: That institution significantly increased holdings
P5: That institution is a Top Buyer

→ Strong bullish signal. A large institution is putting real money in, triple-confirmed.

Scenario 2: Contradictory signals (needs deeper analysis)

P2: An institution is the Top 3 holder
P3: That institution slightly reduced holdings
P6: But that institution is not a Top Seller (reduction too small)

→ Probably normal portfolio rebalancing, not necessarily bearish. Large size makes rapid rebalancing difficult.

Scenario 3: Danger signal (warning)

P4: An activist institution holds a competitor
P7: That activist established a new position this period (first entry)

→ High attention -- activist entry often means change is coming. Immediately check 13D Item 4 for intent.

Scenario 4: Industry turning signal

P8: Passive capital share rising, active capital share falling
P9: Growth investors reducing, value investors increasing

→ The industry is shifting from "high growth expectations" to "value reassessment." Company fundamentals may be entering a mature phase.

Correct report reading sequence (for the boss)

When colleagues in strategic investment receive a cross-holding report, don't read it cover to cover. Follow a "fast first, deep later" rhythm: 

30-second overview (to decide whether to dig in):
  1. Summary cards: number of institutions covered, new positions, liquidations
  2. Who are the Top 3 Holders

2-minute quick assessment (to gauge direction):
  3. Top 5 Buyers / Sellers
  4. Any new Activist entries
  5. Active vs Passive capital trend

5-minute deep analysis (to cross-validate):
  6. Top 5 Initiations + Top 5 Liquidations
  7. Cross-page cross-validation (Scenarios 1-4)
  8. Style / Turnover trend changes

15-minute research:
  9. Deep dive on a specific institution (view its full position changes)
  10. Cross-reference with 8-K / Earnings Call (institutions are adding, but what is management saying?)

Your Dashboard and report design should support this reading sequence -- place summary cards, Top Holders, and Buyers/Sellers in the most prominent positions, with deep data hidden in tabs.

Anti-pattern in design: Putting all data together with a label "Toggle to show details." This forces the user to assemble the report themselves. Good design puts 80% of insight in 20% of the layout -- letting the reader see the direction in 30 seconds and make a judgment in 5 minutes.

6 design principles learned from the IHS report

  1. Each page answers one question. Don't pile everything onto one page.
  2. Leave Commentary space at the bottom of each page. Analysts need to write judgments; the system provides only data. The empty row that a machine can't fill = space for human judgment.
  3. Table columns should align with IHS column definitions. Style, Turnover, Peer Average -- all essential. This is the industry's common language; deviation confuses readers.
  4. Ranking tables must specify the ranking metric clearly. "Ranked by what" must be in the title -- by market cap? By change amount? By share count change?
  5. Charts and tables complement each other. Pie charts for distribution, bar charts for capital flows -- they cross-validate, not duplicate.
  6. Disclaimer must be visible. Not "a tiny line at the end of the document" -- but "data as of [date]" and data limitation notes at the bottom of every page.

Output quality checklist

Before delivering to the client or your boss, check each item:

  1. ☐ Are all monetary units consistent ($M)?
  2. ☐ Are all rankings sorted by the correct metric (not alphabetical, not by CIK)?
  3. ☐ Are there any rows with institution_name = None (collection failure)?
  4. ☐ Are there any delta_pct = inf or NaN (unhandled division by zero)?
  5. ☐ Do both Buyers and Sellers have data? (If one side is completely empty, 99% chance it's a bug)
  6. ☐ Are the previous and current periods actually adjacent (not skipping periods)?
  7. ☐ Are all disclaimers visible in the report (not just in code comments)?
  8. ☐ Is data staleness reflected in the report date? ("Data as of 2026-03-31" vs "Report generated 2026-06-17")
  9. ☐ Has the 13D filing Item 4 text been manually reviewed (not displayed raw)?
  10. ☐ Are Chinese competitors (UXIN/ATHM) marked "Section 16 Not Applicable" rather than "No Data"?

A real cross-page signal stacking for Carvana (in practice)

Below is our actual analysis of Carvana for 2026Q1, using data from four pages for cross-validation:

PageObserved Signal
P2 (Top Holders)Vanguard holdings $1.92B (Top 1), BlackRock holdings $1.53B (Top 2)
P3 (Changes)Vanguard shares unchanged (12.8M → 12.8M)
P5 (Top Buyers)Baupost Group added $480M position
P4 (Activists)No new activist entries

Analysis:

  • Vanguard and BlackRock are passive index capital (mutual funds + ETFs); their unchanged holdings = normal index rebalancing pace, no panic buying or selling
  • Baupost's $480M addition is a significant signal -- Baupost is a well-known value investor (previously held Carvana distressed bonds in 2022), suggesting value investors are re-evaluating Carvana positively
  • No activist entry = no near-term control change risk

Composite judgment: Carvana's 2026Q1 institutional holding profile is "stable index capital + value investors re-entering + no activist disruption" -- a moderately positive state. Combined with the gross margin improvement signal from Lesson 0002, we can preliminarily judge that Carvana is undergoing a value revaluation.

This judgment cannot be reached from any single page alone -- it is the result of cross-page cross-validation. That is the true value of cross-holding analysis.

Quiz

Q1: In the P5 Top Buyers ranking, BlackRock added $50M, Vanguard added $0, and a small institution established its first position of $200M. Who should rank first?

Click to see answer

It depends on the ranking metric. If ranked by "absolute addition amount": BlackRock $50M > small institution $200M = wrong conclusion (the small institution's $200M is larger than BlackRock's $50M, but BlackRock is passive index capital; $50M could be a passive result of index rebalancing with no signal value; the small institution's $200M first position = strong active signal). Correct approach: rank by "active addition amount" (excluding passive index capital), or show two rankings side by side -- one by absolute amount, one by "active new" amount. IHS P5 typically uses the second approach.

Q2: Top Buyers is $300M and Top Sellers is $0. Is this "one-way flow" normal?

Click to see answer

Not normal -- 99% chance it's a bug. Under normal conditions, institutional holding changes for a listed company are bi-directional: if someone buys, someone sells (unless there is a large block trade). If Top Sellers is completely 0, common causes: (1) cross-period comparison bug (previous period data not fetched, so all "decreases" are identified as "liquidations" rather than "sellers"); (2) field mapping error (should have filled value but filled shares instead); (3) data source with partial omissions. Add this to your checklist: "Do both Buyers and Sellers have data?" -- 99% of "one-way flows" are collection bugs.

Q3: In the P4 Activists list, you suddenly see a hedge fund that is not on the ACTIVIST_INSTITUTIONS static list, establishing a 5.2% position in CVNA. What is your judgment process?

Click to see answer

Three steps: (1) verify filing authenticity → (2) extract Item 4 for intent → (3) assess potential impact on CVNA. (1) Check the specific 13D content on SEC EDGAR, confirm it is a 13D (active) not a 13G (passive), verify CIK validity; (2) carefully read Item 4 for the activist's specific demands (spin-off? replace CEO? push M&A?); (3) if the demand involves a spin-off or M&A push, CVNA's stock price will react sharply in the short term -- prepare contingency plans immediately. Also add this institution to the ACTIVIST_INSTITUTIONS static list so the system can recognize it next time. This workflow should be implemented in the Dashboard as "alert + link to 13D," not as a manual process each time.
