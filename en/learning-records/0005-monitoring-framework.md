## Key insights

1. **Four levels of competitor monitoring**: Financial statements (what) → Event-driven (when what) → Management signals (why) → Cross-company comparison (so what). The system handles the first three; the person handles the fourth.
2. **Rationale for selecting the 5 competitors** -- each has a distinct role:
   - Carvana: test whether the "pure online model" is viable
   - Carmax: efficiency benchmark for the offline optimal model
   - AutoNation: observation window into the upstream part of the industry chain (new cars → used cars)
   - Uxin: sample of China's unique market issues
   - Autohome: barometer of industry traffic
3. **Cross-company cross-validation is more valuable than single-company trend analysis** -- Signal matrix: when Company A's metric X changes AND Company B's metric Y changes simultaneously → conclusion Z can be drawn
4. **Reflexivity perspective**: Your own filings are also being monitored by competitors -- every time you write a filing, think "what would a competitor do after reading this?"
5. **The system's ultimate value**: Turn raw data into structured intelligence → people turn intelligence into strategic judgments for their own company
