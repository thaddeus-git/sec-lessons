## Key insights

1. **The core of 8-K judgment is "signal vs noise"** -- the system is responsible for not missing anything; the person is responsible for not being wrong
2. **Three-factor method**: Nature of event × Frequency and pattern × Impact chain on the business
3. **5.02 (executive changes) is the most important 8-K item** -- CFO/CEO changes escalate immediately; ordinary VP changes are recorded for observation
4. **Multiple Items appearing simultaneously = escalation** -- 5.02 + 4.01 (auditor change) = financial problems likely
5. **2.02 (earnings release) itself is not important** -- what matters is whether the numbers in the press release deviate from expectations by >20%

## Three common mistakes

1. Confusing "having events" with "having important events"
2. Form 144 insider selling is not necessarily negative (could be 10b5-1 plan trading)
3. Don't assume you know before others -- SEC data is public, everyone receives it at the same time
