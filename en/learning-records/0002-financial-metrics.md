# Learning Record 0002 — Core Financial Metrics for the Used Car Industry

**Date**: 2026-06-16
**Lesson**: 0002-financial-metrics-used-car.html
**Status**: Completed

## Key insights

1. **Revenue structure matters more than total revenue** -- changes in the mix of retail vs wholesale vs finance often reveal shifts in business health. Rising wholesale share = problems in retail.
2. **Gross Margin is the holy grail of the used car industry** -- it reflects the true spread between buying and selling a car, undisturbed by one-time expenses. Normal industry range: 15-20%.
3. **SG&A/Revenue ratio is the core efficiency metric** -- declining shows economies of scale materializing; rising means burning cash for growth.
4. **Inventory turnover is more important for used car companies than for general retail** -- cars depreciate; inventory overhang = forced price cuts = margin collapse.
5. **Red flags on the balance sheet** -- Cash/quarterly revenue < 0.3, debt growth > revenue growth → liquidity risk.
