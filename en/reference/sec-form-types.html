SEC Form Types Quick Reference

Core Disclosures (essential for competitor monitoring)

FormNameContentFrequencyCompetitor Monitoring Focus
10-KAnnual ReportAudited full annual report: business description, risk factors, MD&A, three financial statementsAnnualMost complete information source; starting point for understanding a company
10-QQuarterly ReportUnaudited quarterly financial data + period updatesQuarterlyTrack performance trends; compare with year-ago period to find inflection points
8-KCurrent ReportAny material event: earnings release(2.02), executive changes(5.02), acquisitions(2.01), contracts(1.01), etc.UnscheduledFastest access to competitor strategic moves; Item number determines importance

Common 8-K Item Numbers

ItemEventSeverityCompetitor Monitoring Interpretation
1.01Entry into material definitive agreement🔴New customer, new supplier, strategic partnership -- could reshape competitive landscape
1.02Termination of material definitive agreement🔴Lost a major customer? Supplier issues?
2.01Completion of acquisition or disposition of assets🔴Buying a company, selling a business -- strategic direction shift
2.02Results of operations and financial condition🔴See key financials before 10-Q; earnings warnings
2.03Creation of direct financial obligation🟡Borrowed a large amount, guaranteed something
2.05Costs associated with exit or disposal activities🟡Asset impairment, layoffs/restructuring -- could mean trouble
3.01Notice of delisting or failure to satisfy listing rule🔴Getting delisted? This is a major risk signal
4.01Change in registrant's certifying accountant🔴Auditor resigned or was replaced -- often means financial problems
5.02Departure of directors or principal officers🔴CEO/CFO/COO departure or appointment -- personnel earthquake
5.03Amendments to articles of incorporation or bylaws🟡Changes to corporate charter -- possible governance structure changes
5.07Submission of matters to a vote of security holders🟡Shareholder meeting results -- check for conflicts between shareholders and board
7.01Regulation FD disclosure🟢Company proactively discloses information (usually accompanying a press release)
9.01Financial statements and exhibits🟢Usually supporting documents for 10-K/Q or 2.02; not important

Insider Trading & Holdings (auxiliary signals)

FormNameContentCompetitor Monitoring Focus
Form 4Statement of Changes in Beneficial OwnershipExecutives/directors/large shareholders buy or sell company stock, disclosed within 2 business days. Includes Table I (direct holdings) and Table II (options/derivatives)Focus on Code P (open market purchase) and Code S (open market sale). Code F (tax withholding) gets low weight (0.2); Code A (award) is not an active trade. Watch out for 10b5-1 plan bulk selling
Form 3Initial Statement of Beneficial OwnershipNew executives/directors/large shareholders disclose holdings for the first time, filed within 10 days of becoming an insiderUnderstand management incentive structure (holdings = alignment with shareholder interests)
Form 5Annual Statement of Beneficial OwnershipFiled within 45 days after fiscal year end; supplemental disclosure of exempt transactions (small trades not reported on Form 4)Usually not important, but may hide "small trades that were forgotten" -- periodic scanning can yield surprises
Form 144Notice of Proposed Sale of SecuritiesLarge shareholder/insider advance notice of planned sale of ≥5,000 shares or ≥$50,000; valid for 90 daysEarly warning of potential selling pressure. ⚠️ Not "already sold," but "preparing to sell." Multiple consecutive Form 144 filings = sustained selling signal
SC 13DBeneficial Ownership Report (Active)"Active investor" with ownership exceeding 5%, filed within 10 days. Item 4 states intent ("we intend to push for a spin-off," etc.) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very high value. An activist is targeting this company? Item 4 language determines signal strength (specific actions vs vague "undervalued")
SC 13GBeneficial Ownership Report (Passive)"Passive investor" with ownership exceeding 5% (pure investment, no intervention), filed annually Understand who holds large positions (Vanguard/BlackRock, etc.). Small changes, weak signal
13FInstitutional Investment Manager Holdings ReportInstitutions with assets under management >$100M disclose holdings quarterly (within 45 days of quarter end) Core data source for cross-holding analysis. Note: value_x1000 field name = "in thousands of dollars"; 1000x misplacement is a classic bug. For period-over-period comparison, look at share count, not market value

13F Unit Conversion Trap (see Lesson 0006 for details)

⚠️ value_x1000 field: Unit is thousands of dollars, not dollars or millions.
value_x1000 = 1,920,000 → Actual amount = 1,920,000 × $1,000 = $1.92B
Verification: shares × stock price at time should approximately match value_x1000 × 1000 (allow ±5% error)
Misplacing by 1000x = classic bug. Keep SEC raw units internally; divide by 1000 for $M display.

Other Common Types (know the names)

FormPurpose
S-1IPO registration statement -- filed when a company first goes public. Must-read if researching a soon-to-be-public competitor.
S-4Merger registration statement -- for stock-for-stock M&A transactions.
DEF 14AProxy statement -- contains governance information including executive compensation, board composition.
20-FAnnual report for foreign private issuers (foreign companies use 20-F instead of 10-K). UXIN's 20-F is typically filed in late April.
6-KInterim report for foreign private issuers -- equivalent to 8-K, filed when material events occur in the home country. UXIN and ATHM file this.
Special note for foreign companies: Uxin (UXIN) and Autohome (ATHM) are not U.S. domestic companies. They use 20-F (annual report) instead of 10-K, and 6-K (current report) instead of 8-K.
Our competitor monitoring system automatically handles this difference -- you do not need to distinguish them manually.
